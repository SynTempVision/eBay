### Purpose:
- To define the standardized process for arranging freight (LTL) shipments.
- To ensure proper documentation, scheduling, and communication.

This procedure applies when:
- Item weight or dimensions require palletized freight shipping
- Item exceeds parcel carrier limits (UPS / USPS)

---

### Freight Pre-Checklist

__ Where is the shipment originating from?  
__ Confirm pickup address and contact person  
__ Confirm delivery address and contact person  
__ Weight (total pallet weight)  
__ Dimensions (L x W x H)  
__ Is the item palletized?  
__ Does shipment include pallet?  
__ Pallet condition acceptable?  

---

### Shipment Details

__ What is being shipped?  
__ Freight class (if known)  
__ NMFC required?  
__ Is lift gate required at pickup?  
__ Is lift gate required at delivery?  
__ Residential delivery?  
__ Limited access location?  
__ Is insurance required?  
__ Declared value confirmed?  

---

---

### Documentation

__ Generate Bill of Lading (BOL)  
__ Verify shipper and consignee information  
__ Confirm freight class and weight accuracy  
__ Send BOL to onsite contact  
__ Confirm onsite contact received BOL  
__ Attach BOL to shipment  

---

### Final Verification

__ Pallet secure and shrink wrapped  
__ Corners protected  
__ No overhang beyond pallet edges  
__ BOL attached  
__ Pickup confirmed with carrier  
__ Tracking / PRO number recorded  

---

### Rules & Constraints

- No freight shipment without confirmed weight and dimensions.
- No shipment scheduled without verified onsite contact.
- No pallet overhang.
- No freight released without BOL.
- Insurance required for high-value items.
